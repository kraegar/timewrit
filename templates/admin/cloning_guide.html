Cloning & Copying Guide

Data Independence

The Timeline application allows you to take data from other researchers (like the public 'aakens' collection) and create independent copies in your own collection. This ensures you can edit descriptions, add private notes, or change dates without affecting the shared source of truth.

🚀 New: Full Deep Copy for Timelines
Timelines can now be "Deep Copied," which automatically replicates every event within that timeline into your own collection.

Copying Options

Action Behavior Best Used For...
💾 Save a Copy Found in the detail view of items you don't own. Makes an instant standalone copy. When you're browsing research and think "I want this in my collection."
🚀 Full Deep Copy Available as an Admin Action for Timelines. Duplicates the timeline AND all its events. Starting a new research branch based on an existing project.
📋 Quick Copy Found in list views. A simple duplication of the selected record(s). Bulk-copying People or Locations to your private database.

How to Create Your Copy

  • From a Detail View: Click the green "Save a Copy" button at the top of any record owned by another user.
  • From a List View: Scroll to the "Copy" column and click "Quick Copy".
  • In Bulk: Select multiple items via checkboxes, choose "Quick Copy selected to my collection" in the Actions dropdown, and click Go.

Collaboration Best Practices

By default, the main view at / shows research from all users. Only create a copy if you intend to:

  1. Add private biographical notes that shouldn't be shared.
  2. Adjust dates or descriptions based on your own specialized research.
  3. Combine events from multiple sources into a new, unique timeline.
